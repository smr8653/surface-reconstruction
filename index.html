Feature Preserving Surface Reconstruction

Author: Sherry Robinson

Description

Introduction to surface reconstruction methods and objectives.

Surface reconstruction is a challenging open problem in the field of computational geometry. Given a sufficiently dense sample of points, a reconstruction of the surface homeomorphic to the original has been accomplished for smooth surfaces. However, this problem remains open for surfaces with sharp edges or corners. This project will involve researching existing methods of smooth and non-smooth surface reconstruction and investigating methods of ensuring a reconstruction of a surface with sharp edges/corners is homeomorphic to the original. This problem has applications in 3D scanning of objects to make accurate virtual models which is relevant to many fields including robotic navigation and manipulation of objects, computer graphics, virtual models, etc. The end result of this project will be a paper describing the results of the research and investigation.
